#### What are the main Agile ceremonies, and what is their purpose?
* **Daily Stand-ups**: Short meetings where team members share what they worked on, what they plan to do next, and any blockers.
* **Sprint Planning (Scrum) vs. Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)**:
  * *(Scrum)* The team plans a fixed set of work for a sprint with defined goals.
  * *(Kanban)* Work is continuously prioritized without fixed sprints, allowing more flexibility.
* **Retrospectives**: Meetings to reflect on what went well, what didn‚Äôt, and how to improve.
* **Backlog Refinement**: Sessions to clarify, estimate, and prioritize upcoming work.

#### How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?
Agile teams collaborate using written updates, shared task boards, documentation, and recorded meetings. These tools allow everyone to stay aligned without needing to be online at the same time.

Agile ceremonies provide structured and recurring opportunities to share progress, surface issues early, and align on priorities. This helps reduce misunderstandings and keeps the team focused on common goals.

#### Participate in a retrospective or review past notes from one.

After the stand-up, notes from the meeting are automatically generated using an AI tool and sent to the team by email. These notes summarize what each team member shared and help keep track of progress asynchronously.

In addition to the AI-generated notes, I sometimes take my own notes using Notepad or Notion to keep a personal record of tasks completed and next steps. Reviewing these notes helps me stay aligned with team goals and remember feedback or follow-up actions discussed during the meeting.
