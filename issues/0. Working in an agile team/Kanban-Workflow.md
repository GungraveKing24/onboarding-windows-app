#### How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
Board vizualizes work as taks moving trough defined stages. It helps manage workflow by making progress visible.
Help to identify bottlenecks, and ensuring work is completed step by step.

#### What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent? (e.g., Backlog, In Progress, Blocked, Done)
* Backlog: Tasks that are planned but but not yet started
* In progress: Tasks currently being worked on
* Bloqued: Tasks that can not move foward foward to an issue
* Done: Tasks completed

#### How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks move from left to right as a work progress. The person working on the taks ir responsable to updating its status

#### What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
* Help the team focus
* Avoid overloads
* Improves overall quality and delivery speed
